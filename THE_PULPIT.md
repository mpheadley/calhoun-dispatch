## The Name

**Lead recommendation: The Pulpit.** Pending final lock; alternates kept below for the historical record.

### Why The Pulpit

Five reasons, in order of weight:

1. **It names Matt's standing, not the satirical target.** The pastoral background (10 years) is the project's primary asset and cannot be faked — see "Why Matt" below. The Pulpit names the chair Matt actually sat in. A former pastor writing under "From The Pulpit" carries a credential no kingdom-family or register-led name can replicate. Other names point at the *theology being critiqued*; this one points at the *institution being critiqued from inside*. That's the rarer satirical posture.

2. **It does GT-style comic work on first contact.** Half-funny before the writing starts, the way Green Tomato is. "From The Pulpit, your trusted source for biblical news" — tagline writes itself. Sounds like a real Christian magazine that could plausibly exist, in the same register as *Decision, Charisma, Relevant, Outreach, World, Sojourners*. Delayed-reveal stays alive.

3. **It's the broadest metaphor for the brainstorm's content pillars.** Christian nationalism, megachurch culture, prosperity gospel, denominational politics, missionary culture, CCM industry — the through-line for all of them is *Christian authority and how it's wielded*. The Pulpit names that through-line. Kingdom-family names narrow to dominion theology specifically; The Pulpit covers the full territory.

4. **Cleanest wordmark and visual identity.** Single word, definite article, ownable silhouette (a pulpit is a logo). No three-letter acronym to defend or explain.

5. **Magazine-coded register fits Matt's current strength.** Writing-first now, video later. The Pulpit reads as a publication brand by default. Network-named alternatives (KNN/LBN) front-load video expectations the project can't yet meet.

### Considered and dropped

**Liberty family — LNN (Liberty News Network) and LBN (Liberty Broadcasting Network).** "Liberty" carries political register but no metaphor. Reads patriotic-first, Christian-second; LNN scans as Newsmax/OAN to a general audience, which targets the wrong satire. LBN fixes recognition by slotting into CBN/TBN pattern but inherits the same underlying problem — register, not thesis. Same trade-off Matt won once already with Green Tomato over The Calhoun Gazette.

**Jubilee News Network (JNN).** "Jubilee" is already progressive-Christian-coded (Jubilee 2000, liberation theology, justice register). A reader hits "Jubilee" and clocks "progressive Christian outlet" before the first headline lands — collapses delayed-reveal to immediate-reveal. Wrong fit.

**Faithful Flag News.** Names the flag directly so the metaphor loses lift. Awkward grammar, no clean wordmark, no acronym, no ownable visual.

### Strongest runner-up: KNN — Kingdom News Network

Kept on the bench because the argument is real and may matter later. "Kingdom" is the actual watchword of contemporary American evangelicalism's dysfunction — kingdom mandate, kingdom builders, Seven Mountains kingdom theology, "advancing the kingdom." Naming the satirical target on the masthead is the same comic move as Babylon Bee naming the empire. KNN's three-letter `[X]NN` shape is video-native and pattern-matches CBN/TBN for Christian audiences.

The case against it now: it narrows the brand to dominion theology specifically, fronts a network/video frame the project doesn't yet have skill behind, and bypasses the personal pastoral credential. The case it might come back: if the project evolves video-first and the satirical center of gravity tightens specifically to Christian nationalism (rather than evangelical authority broadly), KNN out-targets The Pulpit on those axes.

**Possible synthesis if KNN ever comes back:** KNN as the brand umbrella, "From The Pulpit" as a flagship column or video segment within it. That's how real broadcast networks work — branded segments under a network roof. Preserves both the network frame and the pastoral credential. Note for future, not for now.

### Other kingdom-family names considered

Banked in case Matt's instinct shifts: **The Sovereign** (closest kingdom-family analog to The Pulpit — Reformed buzzword, plausible real outlet), **The Mantle** (prophetic succession), **The Mountain / Mountaintop** (Seven Mountains dominionism + city-on-a-hill), **Dominion / The Dominion** (names the theology directly), **The Gate / The Gates** ("gates of hell shall not prevail," spiritual-warfare register).

## What It Is

A progressive-Christian satire outlet that reads as conservative-Christian news at first glance. The Onion's institutional voice, applied from inside the tradition, aimed at Christian nationalism, megachurch culture, prosperity gospel, and the commodification of evangelical identity.

**The gap in the market:** The Babylon Bee owns the conservative-Christian satire register. Nothing comparable exists from the progressive-Christian side. Mockingbird and Holy Post run commentary, not satire. There is no Onion-equivalent for ex-evangelicals, mainline Christians, and people watching evangelical institutions get absorbed into a political movement.

**Who it's for:** progressive Christians, ex-evangelicals, mainline pastors, deconstructing evangelicals, and anyone fluent enough in evangelical culture to catch the references. Not aimed at outsiders looking in — aimed at insiders looking sideways.

**Why Matt:** pastoral background = credibility to satirize from inside the tradition. The voice is recognizable to people inside evangelical and mainline spaces because it is one of theirs. That credibility is the project's primary asset and cannot be faked. The Pulpit names that credential directly.

**The unifying thesis:** Christian nationalism is not a single beat alongside megachurch culture / prosperity gospel / institutional racism / CCM / missions — it's the theology underwriting all of them. Megachurches function as parallel civic institutions. Prosperity gospel maps God-blesses-this-place onto American exceptionalism. Bible Belt commerce is Christian nationalism in fast food. CCM festivals slide into MAGA rallies because they were already running on the same theology. The dysfunction is unified by a single project — fusing Christian identity with national/political/economic identity. The Pulpit covers the full field; individual pieces target specific manifestations.

## Tone — Delayed Reveal

The Babylon Bee's tone is "we're doing satire, you can tell." That's the easier register and it's already taken. The Pulpit runs a harder register: **looks like a real outlet until the reader notices.**

- Headlines read straight. Press-release predicates. Institutional voice.
- Bylines look real ("The Pulpit Faith Desk," "The Pulpit Field Correspondent — Dallas").
- The site's chrome — masthead, nav, ad slots — looks like a real conservative Christian outlet.
- The comedy lives in the gap between the institutional sincerity and what's being reported.

**Failure modes:**
- Punching down at individual believers instead of institutions. The target is the institutional behavior, not the person in the pew.
- Mistaking sneer for satire. If a piece reads as contempt for the people inside the tradition, it's not The Pulpit — it's the wrong outlet entirely.
- Reading as conservative satire by accident. If a Babylon Bee reader nods through the whole piece, the satire failed.
- Writing in academic-seminary vocabulary. The audience is in the pew, not the seminary library — name *institutions and figures* (Gospel Coalition, Piper, Wilson, Metaxas, Baucham), not intellectual frameworks (Kuyperian, Reconstructionist).

The test: a thoughtful pastor inside the tradition should read it and laugh ruefully. Not a stranger laughing at strangers.

## Aesthetic Direction

Three reference poles considered: **TBN** (gilded televangelist camp), **modern megachurch / Kingdom** (Hillsong-Bethel polish), and **Onion** (sober wire-service news).

**Working recommendation: modern Christian newsmagazine.** Christian Post / Faithwire register. Newsmagazine layout, evangelical iconography in the chrome (subtle cross watermark, dove favicon), Christian-media ad inventory (financial advisors, Hillsdale, prophecy ministries, ministry-coded job board), inspirational verse-of-the-day in the sidebar, newsletter signup ("Get Truth Delivered Daily"). Looks completely real on first contact; the writing does the reveal.

- TBN gild is too costume-y — gives away the joke before the headline lands.
- Pure Onion-clean abandons the visual vocabulary of the actual target.
- The Christian Post / World Magazine register threads the needle: real-outlet plausibility, evangelical visual signals, satire smuggled inside.

Typography candidates: Merriweather or PT Serif for headlines, Lato or Open Sans for body — the WordPress-Christian-newsmagazine default stack. Color palette: muted teal-and-gold or slightly off red-white-blue, depending on how patriotic-Christian the chrome should run.

## Format & Distribution

### Articles (primary, near-term)
Same shape as GT — short MDX articles with hero images. Institutional voice. Headlines do most of the work; bodies are 200–500 words. The chrome looks like a real outlet. Matt's current strength; this is where the project starts.

### Video (secondary now, primary later)
Repackage articles as deadpan content for TikTok / Instagram Reels / Shorts. 15–60 seconds. Two native formats:

- **Sermon-style segments** — fake megachurch pastor, stage lighting, three-point sermon structure applied to absurd content. Distinctive to The Pulpit's brand and satirically pointed (megachurch sermon is one of the content pillars).
- **Field correspondent segments** — Onion-News-Network style anchor-and-correspondent format. Megachurch parking lots, denominational conventions, Christian conference floors. Bad blazer. Slightly wrong lighting. Complete sincerity.

Local theater friends as actors — already in the brainstorm. The video format is where The Pulpit out-scales GT: a generic correspondent reading a national story scales easier than casting Buford-as-Calhoun-bureau-chief.

Strategic note: short-form video is where the audience is, and The Onion's failure to translate to social-native video is the cautionary tale. The Pulpit ramps into video as fast as production capacity allows. Don't let "writing-first" become "writing-only."

### Cast (TBD)
Anchor and field correspondents need names and locked voices, the way GT locked Buford. Defer until first batch of articles exists — characters emerge from the pieces, not from a casting doc.

## Next Steps (when ready to start building)

1. Lock the name (The Pulpit pending Matt's final call).
2. Lock the domain.
3. Write 5–10 articles in flat Markdown, no site, to pressure-test the voice. If the voice doesn't hold across 10 pieces, the concept needs work before infrastructure does.
4. Write the visual brief — pick 2–3 real-outlet references (Christian Post / Faithwire / World) and lock the modern-Christian-newsmagazine aesthetic.

6. Write `LEGAL_GUIDELINES.md` for The Pulpit — different risk surface than GT.
7. Stand up the site (Next.js + MDX, same stack as GT — no reason to fork tooling).
8. Wire syndication: GT pulls The Pulpit's faith content into a "Faith & Religion" section.
9. Begin video production as fast as capacity allows — sermon-style segments first (most distinctive to the brand), field correspondent segments as the format library grows.

The voice test (step 3) is the gate. Everything downstream depends on the voice holding. If it doesn't, the rest is wasted work.
