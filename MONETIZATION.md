# The Green Tomato: Monetization Strategy (80/20 Rule)

This document outlines potential revenue streams for The Green Tomato, guided by the 80/20 principle: 80% of effort remains on creative fun, while 20% can be dedicated to exploring low-effort income possibilities. The goal is not to maximize profit, but to see if the project can generate a small, sustainable side-income without compromising its core purpose.

## Guiding Principles

1.  **Low Effort First:** Prioritize revenue streams that are "set and forget."
2.  **Protect the Voice:** Monetization should not dilute the "Buford Tatum" voice or the paper's satirical tone. Ads can even enhance the aesthetic if they're the right kind of tacky.
3.  **No Deadlines:** Income-related tasks have no hard deadlines and should never take precedence over primary projects (PetalStudio, Marketday).

## Potential Revenue Streams

### 1. Programmatic Ads (Low Effort)

This is the most direct path to generating small, passive income and fits the "tacky ads like the Anniston Star" model.

*   **How it works:** We sign up for a service like Google AdSense or a competitor (Ezoic, Mediavine, etc., though they have higher traffic requirements). We place a small piece of code on the site, and the service automatically fills designated ad slots with ads from their network.
*   **Pros:**
    *   Almost completely passive after initial setup.
    *   The often-tacky, non-local nature of these ads can actually enhance the 1998-era local newspaper aesthetic.
    *   No sales required.
*   **Cons:**
    *   Requires a certain amount of traffic to generate any meaningful income (pennies per day at first).
    *   Can slightly slow down the site.
    *   We don't control the specific ads shown.
*   **Status: Live.** Google AdSense is set up and running (`ca-pub-5099067013703258` in layout.tsx). Real ads run alongside the fake AdSlot inventory — the tacky programmatic ads actually reinforce the local-news aesthetic rather than breaking it. No further action needed here.

### 2. Local Business Ads (Medium Effort)

Selling ad space directly to local Calhoun County businesses who are "in on the joke."

*   **How it works:** We create a simple "Advertise With Us" page with rates (e.g., $20/month for a sidebar ad). We would then need to reach out to a few select local businesses that have a sense of humor.
*   **Pros:**
    *   Higher revenue per ad than programmatic.
    *   Builds real-world local connection.
    *   Ads themselves could be funny and add to the site's content.
*   **Cons:**
    *   Requires active sales and outreach, which takes time away from writing.
    *   Requires invoicing and managing payments.
*   **Next Step:** De-prioritized in favor of programmatic ads first.

### 3. Supporter-Based (Low Effort)

Allowing readers to directly support the project.

*   **How it works:** Add a "Buy Buford a Coffee" link (using a service like Ko-fi or Buy Me a Coffee) in the site's footer or on an "About" page.
*   **Pros:**
    *   Easy to set up.
    *   Direct reader engagement.
*   **Cons:**
    *   Unlikely to generate significant income without a dedicated, large readership.
*   **Next Step:** Add a link to the footer after AdSense is running.

### 4. Merchandise (High Effort)

Selling T-shirts, mugs, etc., with Green Tomato branding or in-jokes.

*   **How it works:** Use a print-on-demand service like Printful or Teespring. We would need to create designs and set up a simple store page.
*   **Pros:**
    *   Fun, tangible product.
*   **Cons:**
    *   Highest effort for the lowest probable return. Requires design work, promotion, and managing a store.
*   **Next Step:** Not a priority. Consider only after the site has a proven, engaged audience.
