ANNISTON, Ala. — The United Way of East Central Alabama has completed its fourth StoryBrand marketing framework certification, a process that director Shannon Jenkins described as producing "significant clarity" around the organization's core message. The organization first completed the StoryBrand framework in 2019. It has now completed it four times.

StoryBrand, developed by author Donald Miller, is a marketing framework that helps organizations identify their core message by positioning the customer as the hero of a story. It involves a seven-part narrative structure. It is taught through workshops, books, and a certification process. The United Way of East Central Alabama has been certified four times.

"Each time we go through the process, we come out with more clarity about who we are and who we serve," Jenkins said. "The fourth certification really locked things in."

She was asked what, specifically, had been locked in by the fourth certification that had not been locked in by the third.

"The nuance," she said. "The nuance really came through this time."

The organization's current tagline, "Live United," predates all four StoryBrand certifications. Jenkins confirmed the tagline has not changed. She said this was consistent with having clarity.

The United Way of East Central Alabama distributes funding to approximately 20 partner agencies serving Calhoun, Cleburne, and Etowah counties. It raised $1.9 million in its most recent campaign. Jenkins cried at the Live United kick-off event, as she has for seven consecutive years. The two facts are not directly related.

The organization is not currently planning a fifth StoryBrand certification. Jenkins said it was "possible down the road."

"The framework is strong," she said. "We know our story."
