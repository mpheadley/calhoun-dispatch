### FIXED — had real-named living people, now scrubbed

- **Anniston Civility Training**
  - **Was:** Named John Spain and Gene Robinson by name from the 2010 incident. Quoted "Councilor Ben Little" and "Councilor Jay Jenkins" — both real current/former Anniston council members with public ethics histories.
  - **Risk:** Putting fictional quotes in real council members' mouths is defamation-adjacent. Spain and Robinson are public record from court testimony but still living people; naming them in satire — even as backdrop — is unnecessary.
  - **Fix applied (April 26):** Removed all real names. Spain-Robinson reference now reads as "what city documents describe as 'a series of governance challenges' during that period." Council members reduced to "one councilor, who asked not to be quoted."

- **Cheaha Challenge Pavement**
  - **Was:** Quoted real event director Stuart Lamp three times with fictional statements.
  - **Risk:** Stuart Lamp is a real, identifiable person. Even bland fictional quotes attributed to a real person create misrepresentation risk.
  - **Fix applied (April 26):** Replaced "Stuart Lamp" with "a representative for the Northeast Alabama Bicycle Association" / "Organizers." Same quotes, no name.

### NEW STANDING RULE

**Going forward — no real living individuals named with quoted speech, even if the quote is benign.** Real institutions can be named. Real public figures can be referenced as historical context. But fictional quotes go only in fictional mouths.

Exception: real public statements already on the record (court testimony, newspaper quotes, official press releases) can be referenced as such — but not paraphrased into new fictional quotes.
