# RMC Sale to Orlando Health — Source Material & Headlines

**Status:** Real news. Announced April 8, 2026. Deal expected to close fall 2026.

---

## Verified Facts

- City of Anniston has owned RMC. Sale approved by city council + RMC board of directors.
- Buyer: **Orlando Health** (Florida-based hospital system).
- RMC currently: ~2,000 employees, ~200 physicians on staff.
- Becomes part of Orlando Health's "Alabama Region."
- Orlando Health committed to "five-year investment" in facilities, equipment, technology, EHR system.
- Employees will be transitioned "on day one, with their existing titles, pay and years of service honored."
- Pending regulatory approvals.

## Why this is rich Dale Brewer material

- **The hospital is the place.** RMC treated the Freedom Riders in 1961 — the same building that ordered them to leave once a mob gathered. Two of Matt's kids were born there. It is a defining institution.
- **The buyer is a Florida company.** A hospital named "Regional Medical Center" — the regional nature of which has been load-bearing for the city's identity for over a century — is now owned by people in Orlando.
- **The euphemism load is high.** "Strategic alignment," "five-year investment," "transitioned on day one," "existing titles honored." Dale repeats every press-release phrase as a fact.
- **The civic frame.** The City of Anniston, having sold its hospital, must now do something else.

## Article Angles (in priority order)

### A. The straight news lede (closest to real coverage)
Dale reports the sale as if it's a normal economic-development story. Notes the five-year investment. Notes the day-one employee protections. Quotes the mayor. Never mentions the hospital's history, never asks why a city would sell its hospital, never asks what happens at year six.

### B. The Freedom Riders connection
Dale runs a separate piece marking the historical significance of RMC, with a single sentence at the end noting that the hospital is now owned by Orlando Health. The juxtaposition does the work.

### C. The "Alabama Region" piece
Pure absurdity vein — the corporate framing of "Alabama Region" treated as if it's a real geopolitical entity. *"Region's Other Members Not Identified."*

### D. The follow-up
Three months after the sale, a small piece: *"Orlando Health Reaffirms Commitment to Anniston During Quarterly Earnings Call; Anniston Not Mentioned by Name."*

## Risks / Things to Avoid

- **Don't mock the workers.** The 2,000 employees are real people who didn't ask for any of this. Punch at the institution, not the staff. Dale's sincerity covers this — he's not aware of the joke, so he reports the "day one" line as if it's good news, not as a punchline at workers' expense.
- **Don't mock individual patients.** Same rule.
- **Real people in coverage.** The Anniston mayor, council members, RMC executives, Orlando Health spokespeople are real people with real names. Don't put words in their mouths beyond what's been publicly reported. Use composite/fictional officials when invention is needed (Commissioner Dale Morrow, etc. from the cast).
